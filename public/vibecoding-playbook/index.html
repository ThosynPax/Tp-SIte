THE REALITY

You have seen the tweets.

Someone built a full SaaS in a weekend using AI. No code experience. Just prompts and vibes. And now you want to do the same.

Here is what that tweet does not show:

  • The 3 rebuilds nobody talks about
  • The database that corrupted on day 5
  • The authentication bug that let anyone log in as anyone
  • Users with access to the admin dashboard
  • The founder who rage-closed their laptop and never opened the project again

VibeCoding is real, and it does work. But it comes with a new category of failure — not from writing bad code, but from building without a system.

This playbook is that system.
WHO THIS IS FOR

Built for Two Kinds of Builders

>_

Vibecoders

People using AI tools like Claude, Cursor, Bolt, or Lovable to build apps without a traditional engineering background.

  • You use AI to generate most of your code
  • You can navigate a codebase but could not write it from scratch
  • You have had at least one project fall apart mid-build
✦

Non-Technical Founders

People with a product vision who are either building themselves or managing someone who is vibecoding for them.

  • You have a product idea and the drive to build it
  • You rely on AI or contractors to do the actual coding
  • You want to understand what you are building well enough to direct it
WHAT IS INSIDE

The 8-Phase System

23 pages. Every phase addresses a specific failure point in the vibe-building process.

01

Define Before You Build

Use AI to run feasibility studies and write your product brief before you touch any tool. The most important phase that almost everyone skips.

→
02

Choose Your Stack Deliberately

Match your tools to your product complexity. Understand what deployment actually means. Stop picking your stack because an influencer used it.

→
03

Architect Before You Prompt

The phase I have had to fix most on client projects. Learn the minimum architecture work — screen map, schema, naming conventions — that lets the AI build consistently.

→
04

Build in Phases, Not in Bursts

One feature at a time, fully working, before moving to the next. The discipline that separates products from projects.

→
05

Manage AI Like a Junior Developer

You are the senior developer. The AI is fast, has read everything, and will confidently give you wrong code if you do not manage it correctly.

→
06

Debug Like a Detective

Stop panic-prompting. Learn a methodical approach: isolate, read the error, revert, fix. Eight copy-paste debug prompts included in the Toolkit.

→
07

Ship Small, Then Improve

Perfection is the most expensive thing you can chase in product development. Define your launch criteria and ship the smallest version that delivers real value.

→
08

Maintain Like It Is a Business

Post-launch is where most vibe projects quietly die. Build the weekly rhythm that keeps a product alive, improving, and worth using.

→
